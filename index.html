Course Description
The purpose of the American Red Cross Lifeguarding and Waterfront Skills course is to provide entry-level participants
with the knowledge and skills to recognize, respond to, prevent aquatic emergencies, and to provide care for breathing and cardiac emergencies, injuries, and sudden illnesses in aquatic facilities as well as non-surf open water areas found at
public parks and resorts, summer camps, and campgrounds until emergency medical services (EMS) take over.



Entry-level participants must complete the following baselines:

1. Attend and actively participate in all class sessions
2. Demonstrate competency in all required skills, drills, content, and activities
3. Pass all written exams with a minimum grade of 80%
4. Pass all in-water skill tests

Course Tasks
Each week, we will complete a module (unit of study). For each module, you will be responsible for completing
the following tasks described below. This course is different than a traditional online course. I redesigned the blended learning format to place an emphasis on digital literacy skills. This course runs under the pretense that you will
be publishing from your own website using your own domain as a writer, reader and lifeguard.

This course will use unconventional methods and that is okay. This course does not use the American Red Cross
learning system. Instead, we will be using a social network tool called Slack which will act as our learning system.
Slack will act as our class stream.




Blogging

You will maintain your own blog. If you already have a blog, you may use it. If you do not have a blog, you should
sign up for a free account here. Each module will have different assignments. You will publish posts for any assignments that are assigned. You will also respond to your classmates' posts using your blog. You will share all your blog
posts and blog replies in our class stream. You will be sharing all course assignments on your blog.




Writing Activities

During this course, you will complete the following:

1. Accident-incident form
2. Textbook chapter review questions
3. First Aid/CPR/AED scenerios
4. Video-based questions
5. Extension activities




Readings

During this course, you will be reading a selection of high-quality literature online on lifeguarding.
You will document what you are reading using video selfies and Hypothes.is.



Video Selfies

Using your phone, tablet, or laptop, you will be recording video selfies of yourself responding to different
module activities. Your video selfies will be private and only viewed by me and your classmates. Each video selfie
should be a minimum of 3 minutes in length. You may choose to use Snapchat, FaceTime, Google Hangouts, Instagram
or Skype, or any other social media tool to record yourself. Each video selfie should not be scripted prior to your
recording. You will share your video selfies and your video selfie replies to your classmates in our class stream and
on your blog.



Annotations

During this course, you will annotate the readings and reply to each others' annotations. You will use a tool called
Hypothes.is. You can make an account here. You will then join our annotation group which can be found on our class website. You will embed your annotations on your blog.




Exams

You will be required to take and pass three written exams and in-water skill tests which will demonstrate your
mental knowledge and physical ability that is needed to be certified as a professional lifeguard. Per the American Red
Cross, failure to show up for exams or failing exams after the allowed number of retests and retries will result
in retaking the entire course. You are allowed 1 re-test for each written exam and 3 retries for each
in-water skill tests.
